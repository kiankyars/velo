## 🗺️ Segment notes & where to resupply/sleep
- **Seg 1 — EV15 Rhine (Mittelbuchen → Andermatt, 874.7 km, 9,206 m):** a connector down the Main to Mainz, then the official EuroVelo 15 GPX up the Rhine through the Rhine-side Mainz→Worms line, Speyer, Karlsruhe, Strasbourg and Basel — flat and dense with towns — then the *Alpine* Rhine up the Bodensee, Chur, Disentis and over the **Oberalppass** to Andermatt. *Switzerland = CHF, not euros, and pricey — carry a card + some francs.*
- **Seg 2 — EV17 Rhône (Andermatt → Port-Saint-Louis, 947.4 km, 9,605 m):** the official EuroVelo 17 end-to-end — over the **Furka** to the Rhône glacier, down the Valais (Brig, Sion, Martigny), along the south shore of Lake Geneva (Saint-Gingolph, Évian, Thonon), then the French Rhône / ViaRhôna (Lyon, Valence, Avignon, Arles) to the Med. Largely downhill-trending after the Furka → your fastest segment.
- **Seg 3 — bespoke EV8 (Med → Piacenza, 874.5 km, 12,521 m):** the climbing leg. Inland Provence (Manosque, Draguignan) to dodge the worst coast traffic, back to the sea at Fréjus, the Riviera to Ventimiglia, the **Ligurian seaside cycleway** (old-railway tunnels), then over the **Passo del Turchino** to the Po plain. Hottest, busiest, most up-and-down — hydrate hard (your heat/electrolyte playbook).
- **Seg 4 — EV7 Central Europe (Piacenza → Passau, 765.5 km, 6,676 m):** across the Po plain to Mantua (connector), then the official EuroVelo 7 up the **Adige cycleway** via Lake Garda (Peschiera, Rivoli), Trento and Bolzano, then over the **Brenner** and down the **Inn cycleway** to Passau (the Brenner→Passau run is a routed connector — EV7 itself heads east through the Pustertal). Gentle, well-signed river paths between the climbs.
- **Seg 5 — EV6 Danube (Passau → Mittelbuchen, 710.6 km, 4,572 m):** the official EuroVelo 6 Danube path to Regensburg/Kelheim, then the **Altmühl / Main-Donau canal** to Nürnberg and the **Main cycleway** (Bamberg, Würzburg, Aschaffenburg) home — a gentle, scenic finish before the birthday.
