# 🗺️ Route Options — 3 Weeks in Germany & Europe (June–July 2026)

You have **~22 days** of trip window. Realistic touring distance for an enjoyable (not death-march) pace is **~60–90 km/day** loaded, with a rest/explore day every 4–6 days — so a **~1,000–1,500 km** point-to-point route fits comfortably, with time for cities, swims, and weather days.

All four options below are **flat-to-gentle, mostly traffic-free, exceptionally well-signed**, and beginner-to-intermediate friendly — Germany's long-distance `D-Routes` and the `EuroVelo` network are arguably the best cycle-touring infrastructure on earth.

---

## ⭐ Option A — EuroVelo 6, the Danube (Donau) — *most popular, easiest, recommended starter*

**Donaueschingen/Tuttlingen → Passau → (optional) Vienna**

- **Distance (German section):** ~**565–600 km**, Tuttlingen→Passau; **~14 stages**, averaging <44 km/day if you take it slow
- **Terrain:** *gentle downhill the entire way* from the Black Forest to the Austrian border; almost entirely on **paved, traffic-free cycle tracks**. The single most popular bike-holiday route in Germany
- **Highlights:** Ulm (tallest church steeple in the world), Weltenburg Monastery & the Danube Gorge, **Regensburg** (UNESCO old town), Straubing, Passau (Three-Rivers city)
- **Fits your flights:** fly into **Munich (MUC)** → train to Donaueschingen/Tuttlingen/Ulm to start
- **Extend it:** **Passau → Vienna** adds **~320 km (4–5 days)** of the most famous, easiest cycling in Europe — a perfect 3-week shape (Black Forest → Vienna). End by training back or flying home from Vienna
- **Why pick it:** lowest stress, best for racking up relaxed distance, riverside the whole way

## Option B — EuroVelo 15, the Rhine (Rhein) — *the scenic blockbuster*

**Basel/Karlsruhe → Mainz → Koblenz → Cologne → Dutch border**

- **Distance:** full German stretch is a **2–3 week** ride on its own (~**20 stages**); the German Rhine is part of **D-Route 8**
- **Terrain:** flat, paved, riverside
- **Highlights:** the **UNESCO Rhine Gorge** (Bingen/Rüdesheim → Koblenz, ~60 km of **40+ castles** and vineyard slopes — the iconic stretch), **Koblenz** (Deutsches Eck, where Rhine meets Moselle), Cologne cathedral, wine villages
- **Fits your flights:** fly into **Frankfurt (FRA)** → short train to Mainz/Wiesbaden to join the route
- **Why pick it:** the most postcard-famous scenery; easy to bail to a train at any town if weather turns

## Option C — EuroVelo 7, the Elbe (Elberadweg) — *quiet, flat, northbound*

**Dresden → Wittenberg → Potsdam → Berlin → (Hamburg / Baltic at Rostock)**

- **Terrain:** one of Germany's flattest and most-loved routes (consistently top-rated by German cyclists), riverside and rural
- **Highlights:** baroque **Dresden**, Lutherstadt Wittenberg, Dessau (Bauhaus), **Potsdam & Berlin**, ending at the **Baltic Sea**
- **Fits your flights:** fly into **Berlin (BER)**; ride a Dresden→Berlin→Baltic shape, or reverse
- **Why pick it:** big cities + flat miles + finish with a sea swim

## Option D — Romantic Road (Romantische Straße) — *fairy-tale Bavaria*

**Würzburg → Rothenburg ob der Tauber → Augsburg → Füssen (Neuschwanstein)**

- **Distance:** ~**440–460 km** with its own dedicated cycle route
- **Terrain:** rolling (more climbing than the river routes), through medieval walled towns and Bavarian countryside, finishing at the **Alps / Neuschwanstein Castle**
- **Fits your flights:** fly into **Frankfurt** (→ Würzburg) or **Munich** (→ Füssen end)
- **Why pick it:** the most "storybook Germany"; pairs beautifully with starting/finishing near Munich

## 🔗 Mixing it up ("elsewhere in Europe")

Because the rivers cross borders, you can easily ride into neighbouring countries — exactly matching *"Germany and elsewhere in Europe"*:

- **Danube** → into **Austria** (Passau→Linz→Vienna) and onward toward Slovakia/Hungary
- **Rhine** → into the **Netherlands** (finish in Rotterdam/Hook of Holland) or south into **Switzerland/France** (Basel)
- **Elbe** → starts at the **Czech** border (Bad Schandau / Děčín → Prague is a popular extension)
- **Lake Constance (Bodensee-Radweg)** → loops **Germany + Austria + Switzerland** in ~260 km, a classic warm-up or standalone week
