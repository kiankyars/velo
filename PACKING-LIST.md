## 🚲 Your trip bike — Giant Defy (confirmed)

You confirmed you're taking your **Giant Defy** — a **carbon endurance road bike** (the stolen Cannondale Quick 5 is a separate matter; ignore it for the trip). That changes the kit vs. a hybrid/tourer, so this section and the spares below are tuned to it:

- **Endurance road geometry, drop bars, 700c, disc brakes, tubeless-ready** (typical for the Defy Advanced/Pro line). *Confirm a couple of specifics so I can finalise exact spares:*
  - **Drivetrain speed** (e.g. 105/Ultegra **11- or 12-speed**) → determines the right **quick link**
  - **Tubeless or tubes?** → determines plugs+sealant vs. spare tubes
  - **Rack/fender mounts?** Most Defy frames have **limited/no rack mounts** → plan **bikepacking bags** (saddle pack + frame bag + bar roll) rather than panniers
- **Tyres:** the Defy clears roughly **28–32 mm** (some up to ~35 mm). For 3 weeks on paved EuroVelo paths, fit the **widest supple tyre that clears (30–32 mm)**, tubeless if possible, for comfort + puncture resistance. Don't try to force fat touring tyres on.
- **Carbon frame/bar/post → a mini torque key is essential** (clamp bolts have low limits; over-tightening cracks carbon). Extra padding when boxing for the flight.
- **Gearing:** a compact road crank (50/34) + wide cassette (e.g. 11-34) gives a ~1:1 low gear — fine for the flat river routes, but if you'll tackle the Romantic Road/Alps foothills, confirm your easiest gear.

> ✅ **Net effect on this list:** under **Luggage**, prefer **bikepacking bags** over panniers (unless your Defy has rack mounts); under **Spares**, use **disc pads + tubeless plugs/sealant** (or 700×28–32 tubes) and a **12-/11-speed quick link**; add a **carbon-safe torque key**. ## 🔧 Bike & components (verify your build / what you bought)

- [ ] **The bike itself** (touring/gravel) — drivetrain shifting cleanly, headset/bottom bracket tight
- [ ] **Tyres** sized for mixed surfaces — **35–45 mm** is the comfort sweet spot for German cycle paths (mostly asphalt with some gravel); fresh tread, no cuts
- [ ] **Tubeless setup?** top up **sealant** before flying (it dries out) — *or* run tubes with thorn-resistant casing
- [ ] **Low "loaded-climbing" gearing** confirmed — aim for ~**1:1 or lower** bottom gear (e.g. GRX 2× 46/30 + 11-34, or 1× 38–40T + wide-range cassette). Loaded + tired legs make you grateful for an easy gear
- [ ] **Brakes** — pads with plenty of life left (disc or rim); bring **spare pads**
- [ ] **Saddle** you *know* is comfortable over 5+ hours (do **not** bring a brand-new saddle on a 3-week tour)
- [ ] **Contact points dialled:** bar tape/grips in good shape, cleats not worn out
- [ ] **Pedals** (+ the **right pedal spanner/8 mm hex** — you'll remove these for the flight)
- [ ] **Mudguards/fenders** (German summer = rain showers; fenders make wet days bearable) ⭐ optional but recommended
- [ ] **Rack(s)** — rear (and front if used), bolts tight + **thread-locker**
- [ ] **Kickstand** ⭐ optional, handy for a loaded touring bike
- [ ] **Bottle cages** ×2–3
- [ ] **Bell** — *legally expected* on bikes in Germany, and genuinely useful on shared paths
- [ ] **Lights** (see Electronics — these are **legally mandatory** in Germany)

## 🔒 Security (Germany has ~600k bike thefts/year — take this seriously)

- [ ] **High-quality lock** — a **folding lock or mini D-lock** (Sold Secure / ART rated). One good lock beats two cheap ones
- [ ] **Secondary cable/chain** to secure wheels + saddle, or use locking skewers
- [ ] **Photos of your bike + serial number** saved offline (for police/insurance claims)
- [ ] **Register your bike** (e.g. bike-ID / a registry) ⭐
- [ ] Plan: **never** leave it locked outside overnight; ask hotels/hostels for indoor storage

## ✈️ For the flight (bike transport)

- [ ] **Bike box or travel case** — **hard case** = most protection (both wheels off); **cardboard box** = light/cheap (often front wheel only). Decide based on your point-to-point plan (see below)
- [ ] **Disc-brake pad spacers** in the calipers (or cardboard wedges) so a knock doesn't squeeze the pistons
- [ ] **Protect/remove rotors**, pad fork dropouts & frame, **deflate tyres**
- [ ] **Pedals off, bars turned/loosened, derailleur removed or shielded**
- [ ] **Pre-register the bike with KLM** (your flight): bike **must be boxed** (max **176.5 × 23 × 101.5 cm**), **always charged a bike fee** (~**CAD 175** Canada–Europe), **reserve ≥48 h before departure**; turn handlebars sideways + remove pedals. *(You can buy a bike box at Amsterdam Schiphol for €30 — handy for the flight home.)*
- [ ] **Plan for the empty box at the start** (storage vs. ship-ahead vs. buy-new-at-end) — see [DEEP-RESEARCH.md](./DEEP-RESEARCH.md#-flying-with-your-bike)
- [ ] **Tools to reassemble** packed in checked luggage (not carry-on — they'll confiscate the multi-tool/blades)
